/ 01 — Definition

Shorter than a protein. Smarter than a drug.

A peptide is a chain of 2 to 50 amino acids. Insulin is a peptide. Oxytocin is a peptide. So is the signal that tells your skin to make collagen and the one that tells your tendon to repair. They're the body's native instruction set — bioidentical, biodegradable, and precise to a single receptor.

Where small-molecule drugs blast across multiple systems, peptides whisper to one. That precision is why they've become one of the fastest-growing classes of therapeutics in the last decade.

/ 02 — Mechanism

How they talk to your cells.

01

Signal

A peptide is a short chain of amino acids — the same alphabet your body uses to build every hormone, enzyme and immune messenger. At clinical doses it enters circulation within minutes.

02

Bind

Each peptide is shaped to fit a specific cell-surface receptor like a key in a lock. No receptor, no effect — which is why side effects stay narrow and the action stays targeted.

03

Cascade

Binding triggers a second-messenger cascade inside the cell: cAMP, MAP-kinase, mTOR. Genes switch on, proteins are transcribed, tissue behavior changes — collagen up, inflammation down, repair accelerated.

04

Clear

Within hours, enzymes break the peptide back into its amino acids. Nothing accumulates. The signal is delivered, the messenger dissolves, the change persists.

/ 03 — Selectivity

One receptor. One job.

Caffeine touches dozens of receptors across your nervous system. A peptide like Ipamorelin touches one — the ghrelin receptor on your pituitary — and ignores the rest.

That's the difference between a blunt instrument and a scalpel, and it's why peptide protocols can run for months without the systemic drag that defines most pharmaceuticals.

What the research actually shows.

BPC-157

Muscle Recovery

Preclinical

Extensively studied in animal models for tissue and gut-lining repair; no completed human clinical trials have been published.

Promising in animal research — human evidence doesn't exist yet, so expectations should stay realistic.

FDA Category 2 Bulk Drug Substances list (fda.gov), 2023

TB-500

Muscle Recovery

Preclinical

Animal and cell studies suggest pro-healing, anti-inflammatory activity; no completed human safety or efficacy trials exist.

Backed by lab and animal data, not yet by human trials.

FDA Category 2 Bulk Drug Substances list (fda.gov); ClinicalTrials.gov

Sermorelin

Longevity

FDA-Approved(historical / different indication)

Originally FDA-approved as Geref (1997) for diagnosing and treating growth hormone deficiency in children; the branded product was discontinued in 2008 for commercial, not safety, reasons.

A once FDA-approved GH therapy — today's adult longevity use is an off-label, compounded application.

FDA Federal Register determination, GEREF (sermorelin acetate), 2013

NAD+

Longevity

Preclinical

No large randomized controlled human trials have established IV NAD+ therapy's anti-aging benefits; evidence so far is mechanistic and comes mainly from animal studies of NAD+ metabolism.

Early-stage research on a well-understood cellular pathway — human proof for anti-aging claims isn't there yet.

Clinical Evidence for Targeting NAD Therapeutically, PMC, 2020

PT-141

Longevity

FDA-Approved

FDA-approved as Vyleesi (2019) based on two Phase 3 RECONNECT trials, which showed significantly increased sexual desire and satisfying sexual events vs. placebo in premenopausal women with HSDD.

The only compound on this list backed by full FDA approval and Phase 3 trial data.

FDA-approved prescribing information, VYLEESI (bremelanotide), Initial U.S. Approval 2019

CJC-1295 / Ipamorelin

Fitness

Clinical Trial

A 2006 study in the Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism found CJC-1295 produced sustained, dose-dependent increases in growth hormone and IGF-1 in healthy adults over several weeks.

Human data confirms the GH/IGF-1 effect; larger trials on muscle or fat outcomes are still lacking.

Journal of Clinical Endocrinology & Metabolism, 2006

Tesamorelin

Fitness

FDA-Approved(different indication)

FDA-approved as Egrifta/Egrifta WR based on two trials in 816 HIV-positive adults, showing significant reduction in visceral abdominal fat via CT scan vs. placebo.

Real FDA approval and real trial data — though for HIV-related fat loss, not general fitness; use here is off-label.

FDA-approved EGRIFTA prescribing information, accessdata.fda.gov

Fitness

Preclinical

Discovered in 2015 as a mitochondria-derived "exercise mimetic" peptide; research remains mostly in animal and cell models, with human trials for metabolic conditions only just beginning.

One of the newest peptides in research — a promising mechanism, but human evidence is still in its infancy.

/ 07 — Safety & Quality

How we handle what goes in your body.

Third-party testing

All peptide APIs are tested by independent ISO 17025-accredited laboratories. Testing includes HPLC (High-Performance Liquid Chromatography) for purity verification, mass spectrometry for identity confirmation, and endotoxin testing for safety assurance. A Certificate of Analysis (COA) is available for every dispensed batch.

Compounding standards

We exclusively work with 503A and 503B compounding pharmacies that meet or exceed USP <797> (sterile) and USP <795> (non-sterile) standards. All compounded medications are prepared from pharmaceutical-grade Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients sourced from FDA-registered suppliers.

Storage & traceability

Temperature-sensitive compounds are held in validated cold-chain storage at 2–8°C and shipped with cold-chain packaging. Every vial carries a unique batch number for full traceability in the event of a quality concern.

Eligibility & contraindications

Services are limited to adults 18 and older. Certain conditions — including active cancer, pregnancy, and some cardiovascular conditions — may preclude peptide therapy. The prescribing physician holds final discretion on all treatment decisions, and anyone taking medications or managing a health condition is routed to a consultation before any recommendation is presented.

Start with a consultation

Every protocol is reviewed and prescribed by a licensed physician. Nothing ships without one.

/ 08 — Glossary

Terms you'll see.

Amino acid
The building block of peptides and proteins. Twenty standard amino acids combine in sequence to form every peptide.
GHRH analog
A molecule that mimics Growth Hormone-Releasing Hormone, prompting the pituitary to release growth hormone (e.g. Sermorelin, CJC-1295).
Secretagogue
A substance that triggers the secretion of another — GH secretagogues like Ipamorelin prompt natural growth-hormone release.
Half-life
The time for half of a dose to clear from circulation. Most peptides have short half-lives (under two hours), so nothing accumulates.
Angiogenesis
The formation of new blood vessels — central to how repair peptides deliver blood supply into slow-healing tissue.
503A / 503B
FDA pharmacy categories. 503A compounds for individual prescriptions; 503B are registered outsourcing facilities compounding in larger batches under stricter cGMP oversight.
COA
Certificate of Analysis — a lab document stating a batch's purity, identity, and microbial results.
API
Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient — the raw peptide compound before it's formulated into a dose.
